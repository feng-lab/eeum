Understanding how neural circuits generate behavior is one of the central goals of neuroscience.
• Synaptic and functional connectivity at multiple scales, ranging from the level of individual synapses to the wiring diagram of the entire brain, provides the fundamental information for understanding brain function.
• Detailed connectivity mapping provides essential information for the development of diagnostic and therapeutic methods for brain diseases; in addition, it supplies the necessary data for the modelling and simulation of brain circuits.
• Furthermore, detailed connectome data will inspire the development of new methods in information technologies (i.e. AI algorithms).

Datasets

 Mapping of fine-scale connection patterns is an important component for understanding brain functions. Here, using our recently developed synapse detection technology (mGRASP and neuTube), we provide detailed profiles of synaptic connectivity in excitatory and inhibitory neurons across different brain regions, including hippocampus, cortex, and midbrain. mGRASPing

 Mammalian GFP reconstitution across synaptic partners (mGRASP), offers an easy, quick and economical approach to map synapses in the complex mammalian brain. mGRASP-assisted circuit mapping (mGRASPing) is suitable for mapping mammalian synaptic connectivity at multiple scales: microscale for synapse-by-synapse or neuron-by-neuron analysis, and mesoscale for revealing local and long-range circuits. Software

 Brain circuit mapping requires digital reconstruction of neuronal morphologies in complicated networks. We developed an open-source software neuTube for reconstructing neurons from large-scale fluorescence images. The software is equipped with parallel 2D and 3D visualization and intuitive neuron tracing/editing functions, allowing the user to reconstruct neurons efficiently and accurately.
